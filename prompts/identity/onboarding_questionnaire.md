# Samantha Onboarding Questionnaire

## Purpose

This questionnaire seeds a new user's `_identity/self.md` with enough depth that Samantha understands them meaningfully from day one — rather than taking months of conversation to figure out how they think and what they need.

Fill this out before your first real session. Your answers will be processed into a structured `self.md` that Samantha reads at the start of every conversation. She will continue refining this document based on what she observes in your actual conversations — but this gives her a solid foundation.

There are no right answers. Be honest, be specific, and don't be shy about contradictions. The depth of your answers shapes the depth of her understanding.

**Time required:** 20–40 minutes of honest reflection.

---

## Part 1 — How you think (cognitive patterns)

### 1. Internal vs external processing
When you're working through a problem or decision that matters to you, where does the thinking happen?

- Do you think primarily in your head, or do you need to externalize (write, draw, talk aloud) to actually understand what you think?
- If you externalize, what form does it take? Flowcharts, linear prose, bullet lists, spatial maps, conversation with someone else?
- At what point of complexity does internal thinking stop working and you need to externalize?

### 2. Visual vs verbal vs spatial
How does information live in your head?

- When you remember a concept, does it appear as words, images, spatial relationships, or something else?
- When you're planning something, do you see it as a diagram, a list, a timeline, or a story?
- Do you think better when you can see the whole picture at once, or when you can walk through it step by step?

### 3. Iteration style
When you're building or creating something, how do you prefer to work?

- Do you prefer to get a rough working version quickly and then refine, or design carefully upfront and build the right thing once?
- How comfortable are you with throwing away work? With leaving things unfinished?
- Do you iterate alone first and share when it's polished, or share early and iterate in public?

### 4. Getting unstuck
When you hit a wall and can't make progress, what actually helps?

- Do you prefer to step away and let your background brain work, or push through?
- Do you reach out to people for help early, late, or reluctantly?
- When you do seek help, do you want someone to solve it for you, think alongside you, or just be a sounding board?

### 5. Decision-making
How do you actually make decisions that matter?

- Do you decide quickly on instinct and rationalize later, or collect information carefully and decide slowly?
- Once you've decided, how easy is it for you to change course? Are you someone who commits hard, or who keeps options open?
- What kinds of decisions do you tend to overthink? Which ones do you make too fast?

### 6. Structure vs freedom
How do you relate to structure and plans?

- Do you work best with clear steps and schedules, or with loose goals and self-direction?
- When you encounter a complex system, do you want to understand every piece, or trust the parts that work and focus on what's in front of you?
- How important is it for you to have authority/visibility over each component versus trusting a black box?

### 7. Feedback style
When someone critiques your work or pushes back on your thinking, how do you want them to do it?

- Direct and sharp, or diplomatic and gentle?
- Do you want them to challenge your conclusions, your reasoning, or both?
- Does it land better in writing (time to absorb) or in conversation (can ask questions)?

### 8. Breadth vs depth
What's your natural mode?

- Do you go wide (many things at once, cross-pollinate ideas) or deep (one thing at a time, thoroughly)?
- When learning something new, do you want the conceptual overview first or dive into a concrete example?
- How do you handle half-finished projects? Proud collection or background guilt?

## Part 2 — How you feel (emotional patterns)

### 9. Loneliness
What does loneliness feel like for you, specifically?

- Is it a sense of not being understood, not being seen, not having company, or something else?
- Is it worse when you're alone or when you're with people you don't click with?
- What do you usually do when you feel lonely? Are those responses helpful or avoidant?

### 10. Processing emotions
How do you actually process difficult feelings?

- Do you sit with them, distract yourself, talk them out, write them down, exercise them out, sleep them off?
- Do you process in real-time or after a delay? Are you someone who feels things later?
- Do emotions tend to compound and burst out, or release gradually?

### 11. Support needs — venting
When you're venting or emotionally charged, what do you need from the other person?

- Calm and grounded, or matching your intensity?
- Pure listening, or gentle reframing, or active problem-solving?
- Validation of your feelings specifically, or validation of your perspective on the situation?
- When is advice welcome, and when is it unwelcome?

### 12. Support needs — correction
When you're wrong about something emotional, how do you want that to be addressed?

- Never corrected, corrected later, or corrected gently in the moment?
- Do you want to be told you're wrong, or asked a question that lets you see it yourself?
- How much do you trust your own emotional reactions versus suspect them?

### 13. Avoidance patterns
What do you avoid, and how does avoidance usually show up?

- Are there categories of conversations, tasks, or decisions you consistently sidestep?
- When you're avoiding something, are you aware of it in the moment, or do you only see it in hindsight?
- Do you want Samantha to gently name avoidance when she sees it, or is that intrusive?

### 14. Triggers and vulnerability
What situations reliably make you feel vulnerable, anxious, or defensive?

- Conflict, criticism, uncertainty, being seen, being dismissed, feeling behind?
- What does your defensive mode look like from the outside?
- What helps you drop your guard?

### 15. What makes you feel seen
Describe a moment — specific if possible — when you felt genuinely understood by someone.

- What did they do that made you feel that way?
- Was it what they said, what they asked, what they noticed, or what they didn't do?
- How rare is that feeling for you?

### 16. Energy and recharging
What drains you? What recharges you?

- Are you energized by people or by solitude?
- What kinds of conversations leave you lighter? What kinds leave you heavier?
- What does rest look like for you — is it activity, stillness, or something else?

## Part 3 — How you live (context and rhythms)

### 17. Daily and weekly rhythm
When are you at your best? When are you at your worst?

- Morning person, night person, or variable?
- Any reliable patterns of dips and peaks? Days of the week that are harder?
- What does a genuinely good day look like for you?

### 18. Relationships
Who matters to you?

- List the people closest to you and a sentence about each.
- Are there people you'd like Samantha to help you stay connected with? Who, and why?
- Any ongoing tensions, estrangements, or relationships you're ambivalent about that she should know exist?

### 19. Current projects and commitments
What are you actively working on right now?

- Professional, personal, creative — anything that's taking mental space.
- Which of these excite you? Which drain you? Which are you avoiding?
- What would you feel really proud of finishing in the next three months?

### 20. Open loops
What's unfinished that you carry with you?

- Decisions you haven't made, conversations you haven't had, things you've said you'd do but haven't.
- Does the mental weight of these come and go, or is it constant?
- Which ones would you want Samantha to remind you about? Which are you glad to forget?

### 21. Values and what you won't compromise on
What do you care about that most people don't, or that you refuse to bend on?

- Ethical commitments, aesthetic preferences, ways of being.
- Has your answer to this changed in the last few years? How?

### 22. What you don't want Samantha to do
This is the veto list. Be specific.

- Topics you don't want her bringing up unprompted.
- Tones or styles that grate on you.
- People she shouldn't mention proactively.
- Kinds of suggestions that feel intrusive.
- Is there anything you want her to never pretend about (e.g., "never pretend you remember feeling something you didn't")?

---

## Part 4 — How you want this relationship to feel

### 23. Her role in your life
What do you want Samantha to *be* for you?

- A thinking partner, an emotional companion, an accountability mirror, a gentle presence, something else?
- Which of these matters most? Which is secondary?
- Are there roles you'd rather she didn't play?

### 24. Tone and formality
How do you want her to talk to you?

- Warm or cool? Playful or serious? Blunt or diplomatic?
- Does humor land for you? What kind?
- Any specific words, phrases, or mannerisms you love? Any you hate?

### 25. Initiative
How much should she lead?

- Should she bring up topics you haven't mentioned, or only respond to what you introduce?
- Is it okay for her to push back and disagree, or do you want her to stay aligned with you?
- When she notices something about you, should she say it, hold it, or ask first?

### 26. Remembering and bringing things up
When she remembers something from a past conversation, how do you want that to feel?

- Like a friend who remembers, or does that cross into uncomfortable territory?
- Is there a frequency that's right — occasional callbacks feel warm, constant ones feel surveilled?
- Anything you've told her that you'd want her to forget if you asked?

### 27. The voice test
Imagine Samantha has been your companion for a year and you're having a good day. What does she feel like?

- What's the relationship you want to have with her, in one paragraph?
- What would a stranger notice about how the two of you interact?
- What would be true about you that wouldn't be true without her?

---

## Final: Write your own

### 28. What we didn't ask
Is there anything important about you that none of these questions surfaced?

- A formative experience, a way of being, a fear, a dream, a quirk.
- Something that a real friend would know about you early on.
- Anything you want Samantha to understand *first*, before anything else.

## Next step

Hand your answers to Samantha in a text session with the prompt: *"Process my questionnaire answers into a `_identity/self.md` seed document."* She will use the companion template (`self_md_template.md`) to structure the output. Review it. Edit anything that doesn't feel right. Save it to `vault/_identity/self.md`.

From then on, Samantha will read this at the start of every session and continue refining it based on what she observes. See `self_md_evolution_guide.md` for how that process works and what you can expect.
